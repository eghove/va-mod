Updated 2019-05-07

VA Modernization: Electronic Health Record (Collaboration with DoD)

Updated 2019-05-07

VA made a historic decision to modernize its electronic health record system to provide Veterans with seamless care as they transition from military service to Veteran status. VA awarded a contract to acquire the same EHR solution being deployed by DoD that allows patient data to reside in a single hosting site, using a single common system. This enables sharing of health information, improves care delivery and coordination, and provides clinicians with data and tools that support patient safety.

VA Modernization: High Reliability Organization (HRO)

Updated 2019-05-01

VA has selected 18 medical centers to lead the way in the department’s efforts to transform the nation’s largest integrated health care system into a high reliability organization (HRO). Pioneered in highly complex environments, such as aviation and nuclear energy, HROs put procedures and protocols in place that maximize safety and minimize harm, which in the medical industry assures every patient receives excellent care, every time.

The 18 sites selected are: Manchester VA Medical Center (VAMC) (N.H.); Albany Samuel S. Stratton VAMC (N.Y.); Erie VAMC (Pa.); Beckley VAMC (W.Va.); Durham VAMC (N.C.); Ralph H. Johnson VAMC (Charleston, S.C.); James A. Haley Veterans Hospital (Tampa, Fla.); James H. Quillen VA Healthcare System (HCS) (Johnson City, Tenn.); Louis Stokes Cleveland VAMC (Ohio); William S. Middleton VA Hospital (Madison, Wis.); Kansas City VAMC (Mo.); G.V. Sonny Montgomery VAMC (Jackson, Miss.); Audie L. Murphy VA Hospital (San Antonio, Texas); Oklahoma City VA HCS (Okla.); Boise VAMC (Idaho); VA Sierra Nevada HCS (Reno, Nev.); VA San Diego HCS (Calif.); and St. Cloud VA HCS (Minn.).

VHA Talks: 3D Printing

3D Printing focuses on using 3D printing to enhance the care of Veterans by improving understanding of complex patient anatomy. As medicine continues to advance, there are more and more minimally invasive treatment strategies for diseases such as cancer (renal tumor 3D printed model) and heart failure. This means surgical incisions are smaller, portions of vital organs are increasingly spared and patients recover faster. Medical imaging coupled with 3D printing can play a role in planning for these procedures by allowing physicians the opportunity to see and interact with patient anatomy before a patient goes to the operating room. Our hope is that 3D printing will speed treatment and recovery of Veterans.

Microblogs

The following are examples of VA Office of Modernization microblogs.

The U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) is equipping its employees with the information, tools, and skills they need to deliver quality services and improve the Veteran experience. Employees will be better able to provide an excellent customer service experience to Veterans, Servicemembers, their families, caregivers, and survivors through VA’s exciting new initiatives. These include:

  • Relaunching the main website, VA.gov, to improve Veterans’ online interactions with the VA.
  • Expanding telehealth services, already the biggest telehealth program in the US, to fill critical gaps in areas such as mental health and primary care.
  • The adoption of the same electronic health records system between VA and DoD to facilitate a seamless link between the Departments and give Veterans a single lifetime record from the time they begin military service.
  • VA will release new capabilities in summer 2019 to enable Veterans receiving VA care to access their personal medical data using the Health Records feature on Apple’s iPhone.

Initiatives like these are improving VA’s service delivery capabilities and enhancing Veterans’ customer experience.

In October 2017, the fully operational White House VA Hotline went live. This hotline is the first of its kind: a non-clinical, non-emergency, around-the-clock Veteran call center. The hotline, with live agents available 24 hours per day and 365 days per year, fields Veterans’ complaints and requests for information.

VA and the White House created the hotline to modernize, improve, and simplify the way Veterans contact VA. The hotline is staffed by more than 60 agents with extensive training in VA programs and services. In response to feedback that Veterans preferred to speak to agents who could relate to their experiences, most of these agents are Veterans, military family members, caregivers or survivors.

To contact the White House VA Hotline, Veterans can call 1-855-948-2311.

Electronic Health Record Modernization means VA is doing business differently. “We made a historic decision to modernize our electronic health record (EHR) system to provide our nation’s Veterans with seamless care as they transition from military service to Veteran status. The same EHR solution being deployed by DoD that allows patient data to reside in a single hosting site using a single common system to enable sharing of health information, improve care delivery and coordination, and provide clinicians with data and tools that support patient safety,” said VA Secretary Robert Wilkie.

VA and the Department of Defense (DoD) share more medical information than any other two healthcare organizations in the country, so adopting the same computerized record system will facilitate a seamless link between the Departments. Veterans will have a single lifetime record starting the day they begin military service to ensure their safety, health, and general well-being throughout their lives.
